Icelandic brownian shuffle
Before the Icelanders developed fire as a means of getting warm, they used to practice this dance, which invloved huddling close together in a circle on the floor, and shuffling their bodies around in imperceptibly tiny, very rapid movements. Arctic robot dance
An interesting example of historic misinformation, English explorers in the 1960s believed to have discovered a new dance style characterised by "robotic", stilted movements, being practiced by inhabitants of Northern Alaska and Canada.
